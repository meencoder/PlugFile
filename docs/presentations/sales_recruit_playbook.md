## SLIDE 3 — The World Before Plugfile

**How a W-3 gets filled out today:**

1. Plugger finishes the job at the wellsite (often remote, no cell service)
2. Drives back to the office
3. Pulls out a paper W-3 form from a filing cabinet (or downloads a PDF and prints it)
4. Types in well data — looks up the API number, lease name, legal description
5. Calls the office to get the GAU letter depth
6. Hand-writes Section IX (surface restoration narrative) — usually 3–5 sentences
7. Chases down the operator's signature
8. Drives or mails two copies to the RRC District Office within 30 days

**Time spent per W-3: 45 minutes to 3 hours**
**Forms filed late or with errors: common enough that RRC sends violation notices**

> "I've had guys fill out the wrong well depth, wrong county, wrong API number. Every mistake is a phone call from Austin."
> — *The conversation you will hear on every first call*

## SLIDE 4 — The Regulatory Stakes (Why They Care)

**The form is not optional.**

- TAC §91.103: Failure to file within 30 days = civil penalty up to **$10,000/day**
- RRC routinely audits plugging records and issues Notices of Violation (NOVs)
- A bad W-3 (wrong BUQW depth, missing GAU reference) = **mandatory re-plugging risk**
- Section IX errors create **personal liability** for the operator signatory

**The GAU letter:**
- Every well must comply with TAC §3.14 — protecting usable groundwater during plugging
- The Railroad Commission's Groundwater Advisory Unit (GAU) sends a letter to every well before plugging stating the BUQW (Base of Usable Quality Water) depth
- That depth **must** appear on the W-3
- Operators routinely misread or lose this letter

**The hook for your first call:**
> "When was the last time someone on your team had to redo a W-3 because a field on it was wrong?"

## SLIDE 5 — What Plugfile Does

**The 5-step wellsite-to-PDF workflow:**

```
Step 1: Enter API Number
         → Plugfile auto-fills operator, lease, legal description, depths from RRC records

Step 2: Upload GAU Letter (phone camera or file)
         → Plugfile reads the letter, extracts BUQW depth + GAU reference number
         → Even handles scanned/handwritten letters via AI

Step 3: Voice Record (90 seconds at the wellsite)
         → Operator or plugger describes what they did
         → "Cut casing 3 feet below grade, welded steel cap, removed pump jack and tanks,
            graded and seeded, John Smith gave consent…"

Step 4: Review extracted data
         → AI fills all Section IX slots: casing depth, cap type, equipment removed,
            vegetation, grading, date, surface owner consent
         → Operator edits if needed

Step 5: Download W-3 PDF
         → Print-ready, pre-filled, DRAFT watermark on free / clean on paid
         → Walk it to the District Office or hand to the operator for signature
```

**Total time: 5–8 minutes vs. 45–180 minutes**

## SLIDE 14 — Glossary (Know These Terms Cold)

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **W-3** | RRC Form — Plugging Record. Required within 30 days of plugging a well |
| **GAU** | Groundwater Advisory Unit — RRC division that issues groundwater depth letters |
| **BUQW** | Base of Usable Quality Water — the depth above which groundwater must be protected |
| **TAC §3.14** | Texas Administrative Code rule governing well plugging and groundwater protection |
| **API Number** | 14-digit unique well identifier: 42-[county]-[serial] |
| **P&A** | Plug and Abandon — the process of permanently sealing a wellbore |
| **NOV** | Notice of Violation — RRC enforcement letter, precursor to fines |
| **Section IX** | The surface restoration narrative section of the W-3 form |
| **District Office** | Where operators physically file the W-3 (8 districts across Texas) |
| **P-5** | Operator organization report — how RRC tracks who the responsible party is |
