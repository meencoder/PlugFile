# The Regulatory Stakes

**Filing late or incorrectly is expensive:**

| Risk | Cost |
|------|------|
| Late filing penalty (TAC §91.103) | Up to **$10,000/day** |
| Wrong BUQW depth on W-3 | NOV + potential **re-plug requirement** |
| Average NOV resolution | **$18,000–$50,000** in penalties + legal |

**The GAU letter is the #1 source of errors**
- The GAU letter states the BUQW depth every operator must protect
- Operators routinely misread, lose, or transpose the depth
- Wrong depth = wrong plugging program = regulatory exposure

> **Opening question for every call:**
> *"When was the last time your team had to redo a W-3 because a field was wrong?"*

# Glossary — Know These Cold

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **W-3** | RRC Plugging Record — required within 30 days of plugging |
| **GAU** | Groundwater Advisory Unit — issues BUQW depth letters |
| **BUQW** | Base of Usable Quality Water — groundwater protection depth |
| **TAC §3.14** | Texas rule governing plugging + groundwater protection |
| **API Number** | 14-digit well ID: `42-[county]-[serial]` |
| **P&A** | Plug and Abandon — permanently sealing a wellbore |
| **NOV** | Notice of Violation — RRC enforcement letter |
| **Section IX** | Surface restoration narrative on the W-3 |
| **District Office** | Where operators physically file the W-3 (8 districts) |
| **P-5** | Operator organisation report — tracks responsible party |
