Variant D · Strata Seal

The abstract one

Three horizontal slate bars of decreasing width, evoking geological strata viewed in profile — or a stacked plug seen sideways. A yellow surface line on top suggests grade level; the bottom orange band is the cement seal at depth. Most distinctive silhouette of the four; no document, no letter, pure geometry.

How to choose

  • If your buyer is a non-technical Texas operator who needs to immediately decode "this is a tool for filing my plug record" → Variant A (Filed Document). Literal wins for non-tech audiences.
  • If you want your brand to read "audit-grade, regulatory authority" alongside Pak/WolfePak/Quorum → Variant B (Stamped Seal). Most institutional-looking.
  • If you anticipate strong wordmark adoption (people will know "Plugfile" the name within the industry within 12 months) and you want a distinctive favicon → Variant C (P-Mark). Cleanest at small sizes.
  • If you want the most timeless, design-magazine-friendly look, willing to trade some literal meaning for graphic distinction → Variant D (Strata Seal). The one that ages best.
