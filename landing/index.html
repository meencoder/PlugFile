A word about online filing

W-3 is the form RRC hasn't put online yet.

RRC has been moving forms online for years. P-5 renewals, W-3A notices of intent, W-3C surface-equipment certifications, W-3X plugging extensions — all electronic. The post-plugging W-3 itself is the laggard: still paper, still in duplicate, still walked or mailed to the District Office within 30 days.

That gap is what Plugfile is built for. We give you a print-ready W-3 PDF — the cement math already cross-checked, Section IX already drafted, every field already pulled from your RRC records. The day RRC adds W-3 to LoneSTAR, Plugfile submits electronically and the rest of the workflow doesn't change.

Why the math is right

The same calculation a Texas RRC inspector would run by hand.

A W-3 is a regulated engineering document. A wrong cement volume isn't a typo — it's a violation. So we don't ask an AI to do the math.

Every cement volume on every Plugfile W-3 comes from the exact same calculation, every time — the same one a §3.14 inspector would run by hand. The math is explicit and auditable.
